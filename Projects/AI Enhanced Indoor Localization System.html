PROJECT CASE STUDY

AI Enhanced Indoor Localization System

Unprocessed RSSI Samples, Heading Data, Predefined Path Constraints, Path-Aware Fusion Model

An AI-enhanced indoor localization system that improves positioning accuracy in GPS-denied environments by combining wireless RSSI measurements, digital compass information and spatial path constraints through an intelligent Path-Aware Fusion Model.

INTRODUCTION

Project Overview

This research presents an AI-enhanced, multi-modal sensor fusion framework to improve indoor localization accuracy in GPS-denied, high-interference environments. To overcome low-power wireless telemetry limitations, the system progressively integrates heterogeneous data sources across three levels. It starts with Unprocessed RSSI Signal Samples affected by multipath interference, incorporates heading data from a digital compass using deep sequence modeling, and applies predefined path constraints through a Path-Aware Fusion Model (PAFM). Ultimately, this AI-assisted matchmaking process successfully mitigates signal anomalies, delivering a highly stable and accurate positioning output.

Unprocessed RSSI Signal Samples

Unprocessed RSSI signal samples consist of per-packet signal power measurements recorded in dBm, which are inherently unstable and nonlinear due to indoor multipath propagation, non-line-of-sight conditions, and environmental factors. To characterize these effects, fingerprints were collected from four transmitters at seven reference points along a predefined trajectory. The system utilizes a synchronized periodic timing protocol—where transmitters broadcast at fixed intervals of n seconds and the receiver runs a matching acquisition window—to prevent collisions and ensure consistent sample density. The resulting raw dataset exhibits high heteroskedasticity and significant spatial overlap between adjacent nodes, establishing a critical baseline that highlights the spatial ambiguity of raw measurements.

Predefined Path Constraints

To enable structured data collection, RSSI measurements are acquired along a predefined path constraints consisting of seven test points arranged in a lowercase *n*-shaped trajectory. This geometry exposes the Path-Aware Fusion Model to diverse propagation and orthogonal orientation conditions across longitudinal and transverse segments. Each test point serves as a labeled spatial checkpoint where multiple unprocessed RSSI signal samples are collected, framing indoor localization as a test point identification and trajectory reconstruction task. By defining valid connections between the seven test points, the predefined path constraints allow the Path-Aware Fusion Model to filter out extreme signal errors that suggest impossible movements off the intended track.

Digital Compass

Heading data is collected via a three-axis digital compass and rounded to the nearest cardinal direction (North, South, East, or West) to match receiver movement to the horizontal and vertical segments of the n-shaped path. This approach reduces computational overhead by grouping orientation data into four cardinal directions, allowing the Path-Aware Fusion Model to organize Unprocessed RSSI Samples into distinct directional categories. Consequently, the system achieves rapid identification of the receiver location on the rectilinear n-shaped trajectory by correlating the current heading with the corresponding path segment. This simplification greatly reduces computational complexity while allowing the Path-Aware Fusion Model to associate movement direction with individual path segments. Combining heading information with RSSI measurements substantially improves localization consistency.

Data Management Framework

The research utilizes a structured Data Management Framework where an ESP32 integrated with a LoRa receiver module captures unprocessed RSSI Signal Samples and digital compass heading data. These raw inputs are logged into a cloud-based spreadsheet acting as a Centralized Data Repository to maintain data integrity and handle high-volume signal samples. The logged data is then integrated into a Google Colab environment for rigorous analysis, facilitating path-aware fusion algorithms that combine raw signal characteristics with predefined path constraints to reconstruct the indoor localization trajectory with high precision.

Path-Aware Fusion Model

Developed in a Google Colab environment, the Path-Aware Fusion Model integrates unprocessed RSSI samples with digital compass heading data, synchronized via a structured Data Management Framework. By analyzing trajectory history and transition sequences among transmitters (such as Tx1, Tx2, Tx3 and Tx4), the model resolves spatial ambiguities caused by overlapping signal distributions. Leveraging real-time heading and predefined path constraints, it filters out physically implausible signal spikes to continuously estimate the most probable, motion-consistent receiver position.

RESULTS

Research Outcome

This section presents a two-phase experimental evaluation designed to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed Path-Aware Fusion Model. The results show that incorporating heading information alongside predefined path constraints significantly improves localization performance compared to conventional RSSI-only approaches. Through this multi-stage validation, the Path-Aware Fusion Model is shown to successfully reduce abnormal position jumps, enhance trajectory continuity, and deliver highly reliable indoor position estimation under complex signal conditions.
