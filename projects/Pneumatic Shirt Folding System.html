PROJECT CASE STUDY

Pneumatic Shirt Folding System

Pneumatic Cylinders, Pneumatic Directional Control Valves,Electrical Relays, Rack and Pinion Mechanism, Timing pulley gears, Acrylic Flaps,

This research presents the Pneumatic Shirt Folding System for efficient and repetitive shirt folding operations, minimizing human contact. The system utilizes pneumatic cylinders and a pinion mechanism offering flexible, and reliable than a large number of electric motors and actuators. The paper includes an analysis of efficiency and performance under various operational conditions, assessing the work rate of the system and the economic benefits, including the payback period for potential investors. A basic relay system is implemented to control the retracting and extending stages of the pneumatic cylinders. Future work involves operating with alternative and advanced systems where the level pressure applied will be adjusted after the determining stage of the shirt and folded shirt being lifted to be packed in order to reduce the human contact.

INTRODUCTION

Project Overview

This research presents a Pneumatic Shirt Folding System that utilizes pneumatic cylinders and a pinion mechanism to deliver a reliable, highly efficient, and low-contact folding process that outperforms complex electric motor setups. Controlled by a simple relay system for cylinder actuation, the system's performance, work rate, and economic payback period are thoroughly analyzed. Future enhancements aim to integrate smart pressure adjustments based on automated shirt dimension detection, alongside automated lifting for packaging, to completely eliminate human contact.

Pneumatic Cylinders

The double-acting pneumatic cylinders play a crucial role in the system, utilizing regulated compressed air supplied by an air compressor to generate the necessary prismatic motion. This linear force—directed through solenoid control valves and modulated by flow regulators—drives the pinion gears and timing pulley gears secured to the acrylic folding frameworks. This enables precise, controlled rotational motion for the folding plates at varied gear ratios, all fully synchronized by the electrical Relay-Based Pneumatic Control System.

Rack And Pinion Mechanism

The rotation of the flaps is essential for the folding process, and a dedicated rack-and-pinion gear system was implemented to achieve these rotational movements. The rack-and-pinion assemblies are secured directly to the double-acting pneumatic cylinders on the lower platform to convert the linear prismatic motion of the cylinder shafts into precise rotational motion. This mechanical arrangement drives the left and right acrylic flaps, ensuring a controlled, stable, and repeatable angular translation necessary for the initial folding stages.

Visualization of rack and pinion mechanism with a pneumatic cylinder in operation.

Timing Pulley Gear Mechanism

Due to severe spatial constraints on the upper platform, direct integration of a pneumatic cylinder for the middle acrylic flap was physically unfeasible. To overcome this limitation, a clearance aperture was machined through the platform, necessitating a hybrid mechanical configuration. The timing pulley gear mechanism was consequently integrated alongside the rack-and-pinion mechanism to actuate the middle flap. Ultimately, the mechanical drive line for this central section comprises three interconnected stages: a pneumatic cylinder providing the initial prismatic motion, a rack-and-pinion assembly converting that force into rotation, and a timing pulley gear system transferring the rotational torque to the upper axis.

Visualization of Timing Pulley Gear mechanism in operation.

Relay-Based Control System

Pneumatic directional control valves are integrated with relay modules to control the movements of the double-acting pneumatic cylinders in the precise sequence required to rotate the acrylic plates. An Arduino Uno microcontroller is utilized to maintain the acrylic plates in their default positions when no input is received, and to execute the sequential activation of the relay modules based on user inputs. This Relay-Based Pneumatic Control System ensures that the acrylic plates rotate in the correct order, preventing asynchronous movements that could compromise system functionality or pose safety risks to the user.

3D Printed Linear Guide And Rack Joint

The custom 3D-printed joint features a specialized two-piece split construction, departing from traditional single-body designs to overcome severe spatial limitations. Standard solid-joint configurations are physically incompatible with the hardware due to the structural envelope of the 1 cm Module 1 (M1) rack, which leaves only 1 cm of clearance once the mounting screws are in place. By split-coupling the joint into two interlocking components, the design bypasses these clearance constraints, allowing secure fastening within a tight mounting footprint. This precise structural arrangement rigidly anchors the rack to the linear guide, ensuring low-friction linear translation and preventing mechanical deflection for highly efficient rack-and-pinion engagement.

Acrylic Folding Framework

Each acrylic folding framework consists of an acrylic plate, parallel clamps, a steel shaft, and two pillow block bearings. The parallel clamps facilitate the rotation of the acrylic plates around an axis closely aligned with that of the steel shafts while rigidly securing the plates to the shafts. The steel shafts and pillow blocks ensure smooth, synchronous rotation driven by the corresponding pinion or pulley gears.

PROJECT DEMONSTRATION

Expected System Performance & Result

Upon full system integration, the coordinated operation of the pneumatic, electrical, and mechanical subsystems is expected to achieve precise, automated shirt folding with uniform consistency and minimal human contact. Mechanically, the rack-and-pinion assemblies and the 1:1 ratio timing belt mechanism are anticipated to flawlessly convert the linear prismatic motion of the cylinders into synchronized, repeatable angular rotation of the left, right, and middle acrylic flaps without structural interference. Electrically, the Arduino Uno is projected to reliably execute sequential high and low signal patterns across the double relay modules, eliminating asynchronous movements to guarantee operational safety. Finally, the pneumatic subsystem is expected to maintain steady fluid power via the air compressor, ensuring consistent pressure delivery to the directional control valves for effective cylinder extension and retraction over continuous operational cycles.

RESULTS

Research Outcome

Upon full system integration, the coordinated operation of the pneumatic, electrical, and mechanical subsystems successfully achieved precise, automated shirt folding with uniform consistency and zero human contact. Mechanically, the rack-and-pinion assemblies and the 1:1 ratio timing belt mechanism flawlessly converted the linear prismatic motion of the cylinders into synchronized, repeatable angular rotation of the left, right, and middle acrylic flaps without structural interference. Electrically, the Arduino Uno reliably executed sequential high and low signal patterns across the double relay modules, eliminating asynchronous movements to guarantee operational safety. Finally, the pneumatic subsystem maintained steady fluid power via the air compressor, ensuring consistent pressure delivery to the directional control valves for effective cylinder extension and retraction, ultimately reaching the target execution goal by completing a full shirt folding cycle in exactly 5 seconds.
