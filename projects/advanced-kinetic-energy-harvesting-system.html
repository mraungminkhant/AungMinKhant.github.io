PROJECT CASE STUDY

Advanced Kinetic Energy Harvesting System

5 phase synchronous generator, ESP32, MAX471, Blynk IoT Monitoring, 3D printed Linear Guide and Rack joint,Linear guide, Rack and pinion, AC-DC converter

This research presents an advanced kinetic energy harvesting system designed to enhance power efficiency in low-power applications. The mechanical framework utilizes a linear guide and rack-and-pinion mechanism for efficient energy conversion, which is subsequently regulated by an AC-DC converter and driven through a 5-phase synchronous generator. An ESP32 microcontroller serves as the primary data processing unit, orchestrating the power management of the system while integrating with a cloud-based IoT platform for performance monitoring.

INTRODUCTION

Project Overview

The proposed system is engineered to capture and convert kinetic energy from vibration of the machines and human foot steps to turn them into electrical energy, which is then monitored. The integration of a 5-phase synchronous generator allows for higher power output and improved efficiency compared to traditional single-phase systems. The ESP32 microcontroller not only manages the energy conversion process but also facilitates real-time data acquisition and transmission to a cloud-based IoT platform, enabling remote monitoring and analysis of system performance. The mechanical components, including the linear guide and rack-and-pinion assembly, are designed for durability and precision, ensuring consistent energy harvesting under varying operational conditions.

5 Phase Synchronous Generator

The 5-phase synchronous generator is a key component of the Advanced Kinetic Energy Harvesting System, designed to efficiently convert the mechanical energy from pedestrian footsteps into electrical energy with less ripple factor. This generator features a unique winding configuration that provides smoother power output and higher efficiency compared to traditional generators. The integration of the 5-phase synchronous generator allows for better power management and monitoring, ensuring optimal performance under varying load conditions.

Rack and Pinion Mechanism

The rack-and-pinion mechanism converts linear motion into rotational motion and is essential for the advanced kinetic energy harvesting system. By utilizing a toothed rack engaged with a pinion gear, the mechanism allows mechanical input from moving structures to drive a generator or energy harvester with precise motion control. The linear displacement of the rack is translated into smooth, reliable rotation of the pinion, optimizing energy transfer and reducing mechanical losses. The physical construction of this custom gearbox was successfully finalized through the precise mechanical assembly of a 100T Module 1 (M1) gear, a 20T M1 gear, an M1 rack, a linear guide, 12 mm stainless steel rods, and 12 mm flange bearings, all rigidly structured between custom-machined 5 mm aluminum plates. This finalized setup supports compact packaging and variable force input, making it exceptionally suited for harvesting energy from vibrations, oscillations, or intermittent mechanical loads within the system.

Suspension System and Structural Column

The structural column framework integrates a specialized suspension system to serve as a high-rigidity foundation for the kinetic energy harvesting system, ensuring precise geometric alignment and robust support for the integrated subsystems. Engineered specifically to interface with the linear guide and rack-and-pinion assembly, this architecture features an array of M12 threaded rods, linear bearings, and 2-4 mm springs configured to provide dynamic vibration damping and structural compliance management. The assembly is secured via custom aluminum plates tailored to the required geometric profile, which are rigidly locked using M12 screws, M12 nylon locking nuts, and specialized shaft couplings. This multi-component suspension design minimizes mechanical oscillations and structural deflection, thereby preserving kinematic efficiency and preventing operation-induced energy losses while offering excellent modularity for seamless integration with diverse infrastructure.

3D printed Linear Guide and Rack Joint

The custom 3D-printed joint features a specialized two-piece split construction, departing from traditional single-body designs to overcome severe spatial limitations. Standard solid-joint configurations are physically incompatible with the hardware due to the structural envelope of the 1 cm Module 1 (M1) rack, which leaves only 1 cm of clearance once the mounting screws are in place. By split-coupling the joint into two interlocking components, the design bypasses these physical clearance constraints, allowing secure fastening within the tight mounting footprint. This precise structural arrangement rigidly anchors the rack to the linear guide, ensuring low-friction linear translation and preventing mechanical deflection for highly efficient rack-and-pinion engagement.

Custom AC-DC Converter

The custom AC-DC converter is engineered to efficiently rectify alternating current (AC) to direct current (DC) for integration into the kinetic energy harvesting system. To optimize thermal and physical space, the converter's chassis is constructed directly from a heatsink structure, which serves a dual functional role as both an active thermal management component and an electrical cathode. Furthermore, the converter employs advanced switching topologies utilizing precisely matched MOSFETs to minimize power dissipation and maximize conversion efficiency, ensuring robust, stable performance under highly dynamic load conditions.

IoT Monitoring and Data Analysis

The IoT-based monitoring framework leverages an ESP32 microcontroller integrated with the Blynk IoT cloud platform and a synchronized Google Sheets repository to enable the real-time transmission of sensor telemetry and operational status. Through the integration of a Max471 sensor, the system achieves continuous, real-time tracking of electrical parameters, including voltage, current, power, and resistance. This architecture facilitates bi-directional communication, allowing users to remotely monitor system parameters and execute supervisory control commands through a mobile application interface, while concurrently logging high-fidelity electrical metrics to the cloud spreadsheet. Consequently, this deployment simplifies hardware management and accelerates intervention protocols during alert states, thereby ensuring the reliable remote evaluation and operational integrity of the energy harvesting prototype
